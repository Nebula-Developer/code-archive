The Tiger Shark
The Tiger Shark is a large species of shark that is known for its voracious appetite. It is one of the few shark species that is known to eat almost anything, including fish, birds, turtles, rays, and even garbage. The Tiger Shark is a solitary predator that is found in warm coastal waters around the world, and it is known for its distinctive stripes that give it its name.
Populations of Tiger Sharks are declining due to overfishing and habitat loss, and they are listed as a near-threatened species by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN).
Tiger Shark Stats
Scientific Name Galeocerdo cuvier
Average Length 10-14 feet
Average Weight 850-1,400 pounds
Average Lifespan 20-50 years
Habitat Warm coastal waters, tropical and subtropical seas
Diet Fish, birds, turtles, rays, crustaceans, garbage
Conservation Status Near Threatened
