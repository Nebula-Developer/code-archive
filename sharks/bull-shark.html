The Bull Shark
Known for its aggressive behavior and ability to live in both saltwater and freshwater, the Bull Shark is one of the most dangerous species of shark to humans. They are often found in shallow waters near coastlines and in rivers and lakes. Bull Sharks are responsible for the most shark attacks in Florida, and they are considered one of the most dangerous sharks in the world.
Bull Sharks are large and stocky with a broad, flat snout and small eyes. They are gray on top and white on the bottom, which helps them blend in with the ocean floor when hunting. Bull Sharks are opportunistic predators that feed on a variety of prey, including fish, dolphins, and other sharks. Bull Sharks usually hunt alone and are known for their aggressive feeding, especially in murky waters.
Bull Shark Stats
Scientific Name Carcharhinus leucas
Average Length 7-11 feet
Average Weight 200-500 pounds
Average Lifespan 12-16 years
Habitat Coastal waters, rivers, lakes
Diet Fish, dolphins, other sharks
Conservation Status Near Threatened
