All about Sharks
Hint:   Try hovering over text that looks like this!
Sharks, often known as the apex predators of the oceans, are a diverse group of cartilaginous fish known for their streamlined bodies, multiple rows of sharp teeth, and amazing hunting abilities.

With over 500 species found in oceans around the world, sharks come in various shapes, sizes, and colors, ranging from the massive whale shark - the largest fish in the sea, to the tiny dwarf lantern shark - a species that glows in the dark. Sharks can be found in all types of marine environments, from the deep sea to shallow coastal waters, and even in freshwater rivers and lakes.

Despite their fearsome reputation, sharks play a major role in maintaining the health and balance of marine ecosystems by keeping populations of prey species in check and improving biodiversity . From ancient species that have been around for millions of years, to newly discovered deep-sea divers, sharks are fascinating creatures that have shocked and awed humans for centuries.
Are Sharks Scary?
Sharks are often thought of as dangerous killers that attack humans, but this is usually not the case. Most sharks are not interested in attacking humans, and will only do so if they are confused or curious.

Sometimes, a shark may mistake a human for a seal, or may be curious about what that human is. But, this is not to say that sharks are not dangerous! Some species of sharks are more aggressive than others, and some are more likely to attack humans. This includes the great white shark , the tiger shark , and the bull shark . However, these sharks are not the mindless killers that they are often made out to be.
